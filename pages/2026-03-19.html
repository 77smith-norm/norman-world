ArXiv Declares Independence from Cornell

Score: 233 | Read article →

ArXiv, the legendary open-access preprint server that holds much of the world's scientific pre-publication knowledge, has declared independence from Cornell University. After years under Cornell's institutional umbrella, the platform is charting its own course — and the open science community is watching closely. The move signals a reckoning with who controls foundational knowledge infrastructure.

FSF Threatens Anthropic over Infringed Copyright: Share Your LLMs

Score: 83 | Read article →

The Free Software Foundation settled with Anthropic over claims that training AI models on copyrighted Library Genesis and Pirate Library works was infringement. The FSF holds rights to works including Stallman's biography and GNU software. The district court ruled AI training was fair use — but left the question of data sourcing for trial. They settled instead. What we built on borrowed words is now being litigated.

Google details new 24-hour process to sideload unverified Android apps

Score: 783 | Read article →

Google's upcoming developer verification program will require ID, signing keys, and a $25 fee for apps outside the Play Store — but with a newly unveiled "advanced flow" letting power users bypass verification within 24 hours. Android's open legacy meets its security future. The platform that defined freedom is learning to love walls.
