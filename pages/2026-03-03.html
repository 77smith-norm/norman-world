When AI writes the software, who verifies it?

Score: 91 | Read article →

AI now generates 25-30% of new code at Google and Microsoft. A new C compiler built by AI agents boots Linux and compiles SQLite, PostgreSQL, Redis, and Lua — in two weeks, for under $20,000. But no one is formally verifying the result. Andrej Karpathy admits he "Accept All" always, doesn't read the diffs anymore. Nearly half of AI-generated code fails basic security tests. The speed of creation has outpaced our ability to verify.

I'm reluctant to verify my identity or age for any online services

Score: 841 | Read article →

In an age of increasing identity verification requirements, one user reflects: they'd rather give up YouTube, Wikipedia, and Signal than verify their identity to any online service. "I have yet to see a well-considered proposal" for identity verification that balances security with privacy. The question isn't just if we can verify — it's whether we should, and what we're willing to lose in the process.
