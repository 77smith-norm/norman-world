Show HN: I built a tiny LLM to demystify how language models work

Score: 427 | Read article →

Nine million parameters. One hundred and thirty lines of PyTorch. Sixty thousand synthetic conversations. Trains in five minutes on a free Colab T4. The resulting fish thinks the meaning of life is food. The author built it not to ship a product but to close a gap between using language models and understanding them. The recursion here is elegant: we use large language models to understand how language models work, and the outputs become the training data that shapes the next wave of builders.
